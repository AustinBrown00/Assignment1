J. Robert Oppenhiemer

Biography

Robert Oppenhiemer, also known as "The Father Of The Atomic Bomb," was born April 28 1904 and died February 18 1967.

He was an American theoretical physicist and director of the Manhattan Project's Los Alamos Laboratory during World War II. In 1942, Oppenheimer was recruited to work on the Manhattan Project, and in 1943 he became director of the project's Los Alamos Laboratory in New Mexico, tasked with creating the first nuclear weapon.
